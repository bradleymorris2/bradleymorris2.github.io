A Brief History:

Avon Cinema is an independent movie theater on the East Side of Providence. The Avon's Art Deco architecture derives from its opening in February 1938.

The Avon normally screens independent, art house, and foreign films. Avon was origionally as the Toy Theater in 1915. The theater soon closed and became a parking garage until the Avon took over.

In the 1970s the Avon became a repertory house and was known as the Avon Repertory Cinema. In 1983, they returned to showing indie films.

Today it still entertains hundreds from around the Providence area.
